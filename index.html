36¢

36 cents of every energy dollar never reaches a single server.

At the industry's average PUE, roughly a third of what you buy powers overhead, conversion loss, and cooling, not compute.7 And the two systems carrying that waste are the same two causing 73% of significant outages.7

Power causes 54% of significant outages. Cooling causes another 19%. The average significant outage costs $740,357, nearly $9,000 a minute, and one in six exceeds a million dollars.7

The striking part: 80% of those disturbances are generated inside the facility itself.7 The problem isn't coming from the grid. It's coming from your own systems working against each other, and it has likely never been properly diagnosed.

+Why hasn't anyone shown you this?

Because nobody installed a gauge for it. Distorted current passes through your meter exactly like clean power, and your bill reflects every bit of it. Hartford Steam Boiler, the country's leading equipment breakdown insurer, lists energy distortion as a cause of excessive heat, equipment malfunction, and premature failure.7 The waste is real, it is measurable, and it is sitting inside your facility right now.

You can hear it.

Transformers hum at twice the line frequency. On clean power it's a single, pure note. Feed the same transformer distorted current and harmonics ride on top of the note, as heat, vibration, and wear you're paying for.
